Hi! I am Lakshay. I am a final year student at Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS), Pilani, majoring in Computer Science Engineering. I am broadly interested in Deep Learning and Computer Vision, particularly in its Biomedical and Financial applications, Object Detection, Motion Estimation and Multimodal Learning.

My research focuses on developing algorithms for machine learning and applying my methods in order to study how machines can mimic humans in cognitive tasks, especially those related to Vision. My research at Advanced Data Analytics and Parallel Technologies (ADAPT) Lab, BITS Pilani, focused on predicting crop yield using multivariate time series analysis of the fused remote sensing data available from Landsat-8 and Sentinel-2 satellites.

Alongside, I also worked as a research assistant at Central Electronics Engineering Research Institute (CSIR-CEERI), Pilani, under Dr. Dhiraj Sangwan on an instance segmentation framework to recognize highly cluttered instances of contraband items during baggage security checks. Previously, I spent a wonderful semester working with Dr. NV Muralidhar Rao at the Economics division, BITS Pilani, working on implementing an efficient framework to analyse the consumer’s behaviour to significant changes in the market as well as the overall economy. I also studied coherence in sentiment analysis, by using external knowledge to augment the models.

Currently, I am actively looking for research opportunities starting in fall 2022. I would focus on applications of traditional computer vision systems or develop novel control architectures to improve upon them. I am inclined to pursue higher studies and research in this field and further contribute to this domain with my expertise.

I am a tech enthusiast and a huge fan of the Warriors (GSW) and Formula 1. When I’m not working/studying you could find me at the gym or binge watching tv shows/animes.

Research Interest:

My research interests primarily lie in the field of machine learning and artificial intelligence, as well as their applications. I am fascinated by human vision and pretty much everything related to it, though I am slightly inclined towards researching vision as a tool for human-computer interaction and that sparks my interest in object detection and image restoration models. I have also developed an interest in multimodal learning, researching at the intersection of natural language processing and computer vision.
